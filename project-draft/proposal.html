Project Overview

Looking for the perfect art piece for your home that would suit your budget, art style, artist preference, and location can be daunting and hard to navigate. We plan to make a web app that allows for art buyers to browse and search for the kind of art that they are looking for. We want buyers to be able to search for different kinds of artists and also browse through an online gallery. This will allow for many smaller artists to gain exposure and possibly help them sell their pieces. While looking at art pieces, the shoppers can look at an artist profile and also get connected with the artist. This will hopefully create a stronger art community. Art finders already exist, but our plan is to create stronger relationships within the art community instead of an "amazon for art". We want artists have a connection with buyers and potentially see an artist profile, and where buyers can support them and see their work.

App Description

The users of this web application are locals and will enable them to expose themselves to try different art types by artists within their community. This app will impact the local art community by giving them a platform to showcase their art to the broader community. By having the categorization option, local have the chance to discover small businesses or local artist to supports to may provide pieces that they may identify with based on race/ethnicity/religion etc. Additionally, this app offers art options based on medium (acrylics, paints etc.) if patron locals have preferences on art type they particularly want to view or buy.

The users will be able to find artist by specific types, location, and culture. They will be able to filter and interact with their results based on price additionally. They will be able to find pertinent artist information, like where to find their website, where to submit commissions, and where to support that artist. The users will be able to easily find indigenous and more culturally specific artists for their tastes.
